The Spark Within―

When shadows creep and dark winds howl,

And burdens weigh upon your soul,

Take heart, for even nights most grim

Shall fade beneath the morning’s hymn.

The storm may rage, the skies may weep,

Yet all must tire and fall to sleep.

No sorrow clings to life’s embrace

But time shall wear its weary face.

Thy heart is strong, though wrapped in pain.

For in thy depths, a spark remains,

A quiet flame that hope sustains.

Look yonder, where the dawn unfolds,

Its gilded touch on earth so cold.

The world awaits thy steps to take,

To shape, to bend, to build, to make.

Oh, tarry not in despair’s snare,

For even now, sweet life is there.

Its call, a whisper soft yet true,

To rise, to fight, and to renew.

Remember, friend, thy strength is vast,

This shadow, too, shall surely pass.

And when it does, the light shall show

The endless fields where thou shalt go.
